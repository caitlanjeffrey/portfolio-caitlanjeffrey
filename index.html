Welcome

About Me

I'm a California native, that has spent the last 10-years of my life living in Oklahoma. I attended Oklahoma State University in Stillwater, majoring in Animal Science - Agricultural Education Double Major. The pursuing my Animal Science degree, I also spent time as a student teacher in southwest Oklahoma. I graduated from Oklahoma State University in 2012, completing my majors.

Career

After graduation, I moved around the state and settled back into the Stillwater area. I was hired at Proagrica, an agricultural solutions company, where I spent the last 3-years as the Lead Business Analyst in their precision agriculture and agronomic solutions department.

One of the products I helped manage was an iOS Mobile product. The complete product line includes a web and mobile application for iOS and Android. My focus was on designing new and existing features, working closely with developers, researching new agriculture technologies, writing requirements and managing the day to day progress of the mobile applications.

Home

When I wasn’t at work, I spent my time working on my farm and garden. On the farm I raised: dairy goats, chickens, ducks, horses and dogs! In the garden, I cared for: fruit trees, asparagus, brussel sprouts, bouquet flowers and an extensive native plant (monarch butterfly safe) garden.

Today, I live in Davis, California with my daughter and dogs.

Killers Of The Flower Moon
Killers Of The Flower MoonX

In the 1920s, the richest people per capita in the world were members of the Osage Nation in Oklahoma. After oil was discovered beneath their land, the Osage rode in chauffeured automobiles, built mansions, and sent their children to study in Europe.

Then, one by one, the Osage began to be killed off. The family of an Osage woman, Mollie Burkhart, became a prime target. One of her relatives was shot. Another was poisoned. And it was just the beginning, as more and more Osage were dying under mysterious circumstances, and many of those who dared to investigate the killings were themselves murdered.

Penguin Random House Author David Grann


Living an hour south of the Osage, where these atrocities took place, I made it my personal mission to visit the areas where these issues took place. This picture, was taken north of Pawhuska where many of the killings took place. My family is Native American and this story is close to our hearts.
